
#Article: Naglaa Mahmoud
#Type: regular article

Naglaa Ali Mahmoud (نجلاء على محمود,  or , born 4 July 1962) is the wife and cousin of the fifth President of Egypt, Mohamed Morsi and was First Lady from 2012 to 2013. Naglaa rejected the title of First Lady, preferring to be called "First Servant," the "president's wife," or "Umm Ahmed," a traditional name which means mother of Ahmed, her oldest son.

Marriage

Naglaa married former president of Egypt Mohamed Morsi in 1979 when she was a seventeen-year-old student.  Naglaa and Morsi have five children and three grandchildren.
