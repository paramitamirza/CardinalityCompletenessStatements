
#Article: Bosiljka Pušić
#Type: regular article

Bosiljka Pušić (Босиљка Пушић, born May 2, 1936) is a Montenegrin writer and poet. Bosiljka was born in Ćuprija, Serbia. In early 1937 her family moved to Jagodina, where her father opened an independent watch-making shop and she stayed there until her high school graduation. Bosiljka acquired a degree in literature at the Faculty of Arts in University of Belgrade. Before retiring, she worked as a high school teacher in Herceg Novi, where she resides to the present day. She is also a painter.

Awards and nominations

	First place for the story Kavez (Prosvetni pregled, Belgrade, 1973).

	Zmajeva počasna nagrada (2003).

	"Živojin Pavlović" 2004 humoristic stories award for her book "Kako preživeti brak".

	Second place for the story "Kralj koji je pojeo sam sebe" (Novi put, 2005).

	Trilogy Naranče pod šlemom was nominated for "Miroslavljevo jevanđelje" in 2009.

	Novel Knjiga o Vojinu was nominated for "Zlatno pero" award in 2009.
