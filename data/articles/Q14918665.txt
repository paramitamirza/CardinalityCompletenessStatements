
#Article: Sanda Rašković Ivić
#Type: regular article

Dr. Sanda Rašković Ivić (; Санда Рашковић Ивић; born on January 8, 1956 in Zagreb) is a Serbian psychiatrist, psychotherapist and politician. Since 2014, she is the president of the Democratic Party of Serbia. She was commissioner for refugees, the president of Coordination Center for Kosovo and Metohija and member of the Serbian Parliament.

Biography

She is a daughter of Dr. Jovan Rašković. She finished primary and high school in Šibenik and the School of Medicine of the University of Zagreb in 1980. She passed her examination for psychiatrist in 1986. Sanda is the author of many technical papers of psychiatry and psychotherapy.
