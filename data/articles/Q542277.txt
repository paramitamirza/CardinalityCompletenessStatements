
#Article: Liang Wengen
#Type: regular article

Liang Wengen ( born 1956) is a Chinese entrepreneur in the fields of heavy manufacturing, the founder and chairman of the Sany Heavy Industry. In 2011, he became the richest man of mainland China, and was tipped to become member of the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China, with a net worth of about USD $8 billion. However he lost that position, and as of February 2014 he was the fifteenth richest person in China, with a net worth of about $5.9 billion.

Early life

He was born to a poor peasant family in the Hunan province in 1956. 

Career

Liang used to work as a top manager at a state arms plant, before getting involved in the construction-equipment industry. 

He is the founder and main shareholder of Sany Group, a heavy industry manufacturer based in Changsha, Hunan Province.

His personal wealth is currently estimated at $5 billion.

"My property, even my life, belongs to the (Chinese Communist) Party", he answered journalists' question about the conflict between the interests of the Party and himself as a businessman.

Personal life

Liang has a Bachelor of Arts degree from Central South University. He is married and has one child. He resides in Changsha.
