
#Article: Gordan Maras
#Type: regular article

Gordan Maras (born 13 June 1974) is a Croatian economist and politician who currently serves as a minister at Ministry of Entrepreneurship and Crafts at the Cabinet of Zoran Milanović. He is member of the governing Social Democratic Party of Croatia (SDP).

Education

Maras finished elementary school "Vladimir Nazor" and 10th Gymnasium. He graduated from the University of Zagreb Faculty of Economics and gaind his Master's degree at the Cotrugli Business School.

Professional career

From 2001 to 2003 he was a sales and business manager at Croatian Olympic Centre Bjelolasica, from 2004 to 2010 CEO of Maximus Communications, and from 2004 to 2010 business manager of Social Democratic Party of Croatia. He is currently serving as a minister at Ministry of Entrepreneurship and Crafts at the Cabinet of Zoran Milanović.

Political career

Maras started his political career in 1997 when he joined Social Democratic Party of Croatia. He was a vice-president of the SDP Youth Forum (1998-2000), President of the SDP Youth Forum (2000-2002), and member of SDP Main Board (2000-2008). At Croatian parliamentary election, 2007 Maras was elected to Sabor in 1st electorate district. In year 2008 he become vice-president of the Committee on Finance and State Budget of the Croatian Parliament and in year 2009 vice-president of SDP of the City of Zagreb.
