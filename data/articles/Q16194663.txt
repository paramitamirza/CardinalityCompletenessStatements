
#Article: PLO Lumumba
#Type: regular article

Patrick Loch Otieno Lumumba (born 17 July 1962) is a Kenyan who served as the Director of Kenya Anti-Corruption Commission from September 2010 to August 2011., and currently the Director of The Kenya School of Laws since the year 2014. An eloquent lawyer, he holds a PHD in Laws of the sea from the University of Ghent in Belgium. He is also a staunch Pan-Africanist who has delivered several powerful speeches alluding to or about African solutions to African Problems. He is an admirer of Patrice Emery Lumumba, former Congolese president and Thomas Isidore Noel Sankara the deceased revolutionary leader of the Burkinabe people. He has referred to and quoted them severally in his speeches. On august 28th, 2015 the PAV Ansah foundation invited him to speak at the 2015 PAVA Forum on the topic Good Governance, Whither Africa?

The 2015 PAVA Forum

Professor P.L.O Lumumba at the lecture expressed his serious concern about the energy crises that african leaders have allowed to reach such devastating stage. He also talked about the issue of african youthfleeing the continent. He blamed it on the economic hardships and the “misgovernment” from their leaders. Professor P.L.O Lumumba encouraged African leaders to rise up to the challenge of changing the fortunes of the continent.
